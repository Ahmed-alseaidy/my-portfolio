About Me

Hello! I'm Ahmed Mostafa, a passionate Computer Science student with a strong interest in cybersecurity, ethical hacking, and penetration testing. My journey began with a curiosity about how technology works and a deep desire to secure digital systems.

Over the past few years, I’ve been sharpening my skills by studying computer systems, learning programming, and diving deep into ethical hacking techniques. I’ve completed notable courses like CS50 from Harvard, and I’m constantly learning about network security, web vulnerabilities, and exploit development.

I specialize in bug bounty hunting — searching for and reporting security vulnerabilities in websites and applications to help make the internet a safer place. I believe that ethical hacking is not just a skill, but a responsibility.

My goal is to work in the field of cybersecurity as a professional penetration tester and eventually earn certifications like CEH, OSCP, and CompTIA Security+. I’m also interested in contributing to open-source security tools and collaborating with other ethical hackers around the world.

Outside of tech, I’m disciplined, self-motivated, and currently training to meet the physical requirements to apply to the Egyptian Military Academy. I believe in balancing mental and physical strength to achieve success.

Thanks for visiting my portfolio! I’m always open to connect, collaborate, or contribute to cybersecurity projects.
